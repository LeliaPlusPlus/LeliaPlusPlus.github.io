Feminist computing is a seminar style course that surveys computing from a feminist perspective, drawing from the social sciences and humanities. Student-led discussions will provide critical analysis of all readings and topics. Thus, all readings should be done prior to class each week. During discussions, please be mindful: think before you speak and allow other students to speak as well.

There is a broader literature on this topic, but we focus on shorter pieces given the timeframe. Here's an inexhuastive live of feminist and other relevant pieces about computing.
