I'm Lelia (lee-luh), a second year Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. My research interests are human-centered AI, computational social science, and AI safety (i.e. distributional shift). I am an MIT Presidential Fellow and Alfred P. Sloan Scholar. I earned a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science, Summa Cum Laude, with minors in comparative women's studies and mathematics from Spelman College (Class of 2020) where I was inducted into Phi Beta Kappa. During undergrad, I interned at the MIT Media Lab, Microsoft Research, Georgia Tech Research Institute, and NASA. I have been a passionate volunteer and mentor for students from underrepresented minorities through my work in computer science teaching and mentoring programs, including Black Girls CODE, Technovation, Georgia Tech Catalyst, CodeHouse, and the Executive Board of MIT's Graduate Application Assistance Program (GAAP). I prefer no pronouns, but if you must please use they/them pronouns.
